With PostgreSQL's default installation on Mac, the port for remote connections is not 5433, but rather 5432 (you can verify in 'plist' files). Replace `5433` with `5432`. To make remote connections without password ask (`-E`) and `port` options. Use the following query: ```sqlSELECT p.id AS profile_id, pub.description FROM publication_collab pc JOIN publication pub ON pc.publication_id = pub.id JOIN profile p ON pc.profile_id = p.id WHERE p.username='eusougabriela' AND ts_rank(pub.description_lexemes, COALESCE(TO_TSVECTOR('english', c.annotation), p.biography_lexemes))  OVER (PARTITION BY p.id ORDER BY p.visited_at DESC) > 0 LIMIT 10;```This query joins the `publication_collab`, `publication`, and `profile` tables to extract recommendations from the @eusougabriela profile's publications that have a description. The results are ranked based on text similarity (using GIN-indexed `description_lexemes`) ordered by recency of the comment (`visited_at`). This avoids issues with PostgreSQL connection ports to execute your request successfully.
